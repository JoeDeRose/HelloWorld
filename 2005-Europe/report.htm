Frequently Asked Questions

What countries did you visit?

Including the one we came from, we set foot in ten countries over the course of our fourteen-day trip to Europe.  The longest we spent in one country was five days, in Italy (eight days if you ignore our foray into San Marino).  The shortest time was a tie, with about an hour spent in both Liechtenstein (which was about right) and Monaco (which was not nearly enough).

  Switzerland,
  Liechtenstein,
  Austria,
  Germany,
  Italy,
  San Marino,
  Seborga (at least, they think it's a country),
  France, and
  Monaco.

Was winter a good time for the trip?

(Most of the frequently asked questions are not, in fact, frequently asked questions; they are simply the portions of the story that most people seem to find the least boring.  This, however, is indeed a frequently asked question, although it is generally asked along the lines of, "Were you crazy?"  I'm using some editorial license in the rephrasing.)

Winter was a perfect time to see Switzerland.  They have a winter sports industry there, and the country was ready for tourists.  Also, in a Switzerland winter, you will see scenery that cannot be found in any other place at any other time.  I highly recommend a visit to Switzerland in winter.

Italy was another story.  Much like we do at home, the Italians simply wait out the winter months.  The attractions are open  but, because there are so few tourists, more than half of the restaurants and shops are closed.  The street vibrancy is impoverished as compared to what we saw in September 2003.  It's great for seeing art museums without the lines  but if that's not your primary motivation I do not recommend visiting Italy in winter.

What World Heritage Sites did you see?

Those who have ready my previous trip reports know that I'm somewhat obsessed with World Heritage Sites.  The ones we saw on this trip were as follows:

  Jungfrau-Aletsch-Bietschhorn
  Historic Centre of Florence
  Historic Centre of Siena
  Historic Centre of San Gimignano
  Archaeological Areas of Pompei, Herculaneum and Torre Annunziata
  Piazza del Duomo, Pisa
  Pont du Gard (Roman Aqueduct)

All but the first and last are ones that we saw on the previous trip.  But even with these repeats, in every instance we saw things  often spectacular things  that we had not seen before.

Did you see the same things again?

For the time that we were in Italy and San Marino, we did indeed go to places that we had seen before, but we made it a point to see and do things at those places that were new to us.  It was nice to have a second chance at some things that were regrettably missed on the previous trip, including the following:

  Campanile in Firenze
  Dante's House
  Siena Duomo
  Torre Grossa in San Gimignano
  Ercolano
  Baptistry in Pisa
  Seconda Torre

What are the things that you missed, and hope to go back to?

  Neuschwanstein (to see the inside)
  St. Moritz Bobsled Run
  Zermatt Gorge Adventure
  Siena Bottini
  Terza Torre

Are there any pictures you wish you had gotten?

  Snow-covered trees in the Tyrol:  Everywhere we went in this region we saw beautiful stands of pine trees with sagging, snow-covered boughs.  I thought I would have a chance to get a perfect shot at some stop or another  but they disappeared from the landscape suddenly when we crossed into Liechtenstein and we never saw them again.
  The piscina at Bagno Vignoni:  I ran up to see the piscina  a bubbling flooded piazza containing water from the thermal springs  while waiting for Kyle and Billy, but I neglected to take my camera.  I thought I'd snap a picture when they came up to see it  but they weren't particularly interested, and I was in a panic over our rapidly evaporating time, so I let myself miss the opportunity.
