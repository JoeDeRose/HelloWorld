Techie Information

Technology really worked for me on this trip:

  • Downloaded Lonely Planet: Israel & the Palestinian Territories to the Kindle app on my Droid, so I would have the book's resources available to me at all times, without lugging around a paper copy.
  • Set up GlobalEMail unlimited data service on Verizon before leaving, so that I could have full access on my Droid. (Isreal has countrywide CDMA service, including 3G in many areas.)
  • Created Google Latitude "badge" so that friends and family could go to a website and see my location in real time.
  • Copied confirmation documents to Dropbox so they would be synched to various computers and my phone.
  • Prepared documents with English, Hebrew, and Arabic names of all destinations to facilitate asking directions, and copied those files to Dropbox.
  • Saved photocopies of passports to Dropbox.
  • Obtained GPS for rental car.
  • Prepared lists points of interest in Google Maps (Jerusalem and Israel), allowing navigation from my phone on GPS coordinates when the car GPS was not useful (i.e., in West Bank or when we couldn't recognize English transliterations of names in destination list).
  • Used USB charger in car to keep Droid powered.
  • Sent daily updates from my laptop using WiFi connections in three of our four hotels (all except Masada).
