Wrapping It Up

We left the Villas Arqueológicas early in the morning and high-tailed it to the toll road to Cancún.  I was right the first time:  This road is boring.  But today we needed speed more than we needed excitement (and more than we needed 237 pesos), so taking the free road was not an option.

Toll for Mexico 180D
Chichén Itzá to Vallalodid
(MXN)$47.00=(USD)$4.50

  
    

Toll for Mexico 180D
Vallalodid to Cancún
(MXN)$190.00=(USD)$18.20

  

We got back to Fox Rent-a-Car and turned in the Polo.  They checked us in with no dispute or difficulty (I highly recommend this company) and gave us a ride to the airport.  And Billy and I took our separate flights home:  him going direct on Delta, me transferring again in Miami on American.

Cancún (CUN) to Miami (MIA) ...

 

... and Miami (MIA) to Atlanta (ATL)

We landed safely, although we both faced disappointment with our souvenirs:  Even though both were in our carry-on luggage, my Guatemalan bowl had broken and a mask Billy purchased at Cobá had shattered.  But the memories were solid.

James met us up at the airport ...
    

... and Prince and Pepper met us at home.

And although it was nice to be back in a familiar environment, the house now seems a little too big, the people a little too distant, the pace a little too hurried, and the weather a little too cold.  Mexico, Belize, and Guatemala each made a distinct, but phenomenal, impression, and I was already plotting how to get back before the wheels even touched the tarmac in Miami.  We live a wonderful life to see places like this, and I hope it won't be long before I get to share with you my next trip report.
