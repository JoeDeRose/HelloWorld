  • Billy and I started out at the Piazza della Repubblica, renting a vespa that we would use to cover the city that day.  I burned my thumb slightly on the muffler trying to tie the security chain, but otherwise neither of us suffered any injury.  And within an hour, none of the horns were blowing at us.  When we started, both of us wanted the other one to drive; by the end of the day, both of us wanted to get more turns at driving.

Billy and Joe on a Vespa

  
  • First we drove to the Via Appia Antica – the oldest road in Rome, and still paved in cobblestones – and saw the Catacombs of San Sebastiano (where photographs were again forbidden).

Entrance Ticket to the Catacombs of San Sebastiano

  
  • From the catacombs we drove a bit east where we were told we could find the ruins of an aqueduct, and indeed we did:  the very impressive Acquedotto Claudio, which was part of an aqueduct system begun by Caligula and completed by Claudius.

  • Still with the Vespa and still with daylight, we decided to try to find the Pyramid, a tomb for Caius Cestius, who died in 12 BCE.  Adjacent to the Pyramid is the Protestant Cemetery – locked at the time of our visit – where the "Pyramid Cats" reside; according to a sign, they can be visited at certain hours.

Pyramid

       

One of the Pyramid Cats

  
  • We revisited our dining options, realizing that there was a difference between local or specialty food (which might be unlike, or better than, things we could find at home) and nice food (to which we are lucky to have plentiful access at home).  We decided seek out the former and make no special effort for the latter, and to to eat unapologetically when nothing special could be found.  This decision led us to dine at McDonald's in the Piazza della Repubblica, the first of several McDonald's restaurants at which we would eat.  Billy tried a sandwich – not yet sold in America – called the Crispy McBacon.  Later in the trip I would try the 280 gr. burger, which was quite good.  (N.B.:  280 g = 0.617 lb.; the "metric" Quarter Pounder is #4, the "McRoyal Deluxe.")  Notice how the menu items are color-coded for whether they are prosciutto (pork), pollo (chiken [sic]), bovino (beef), or fish.
