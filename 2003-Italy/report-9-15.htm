  • We picked up our rental car early, a Ford Focus, and headed west to Pisa.
International Driving Permit (I stayed awake at the wheel better than for the picture)
  • Finding our way to the Campo dei Miracoli in the "Centro" (center of town) in Pisa was a bit confusing, but otherwise our first foray into Italian roadways provided no difficulty.  After a couple of circuits, we parked the car and made our way to pick up our Leaning Tower of Pisa tickets.

  • After lunch in Pisa we left for Siena.  I was a bit flummoxed by the directional indicators on the Italian roads, but Meridel made a good navigator, and we found a scenic route in our direction.  On a lark we stopped in San Gimignano, a well-preserved medieval town.  We had been advised that San Gimignano is a "touristy hole" – but we found it pleasant, illuminating, beautiful, and well worth the stop.

  • Subsequently, we made our way to Siena and had a nice dinner at a restaurant on the Piazza del Campo; the Piazza is an enormous semicircle, divided into nine sections with slightly different shades of brick to symbolize the Council of Nine that once ruled the city.

  • The walk from the piazza through the "pedestrian area" (which was actually full of cars, vespas, and busses) was much longer than we expected, and on the way back we stopped for a bathroom break.  Here I had my first introduction to a type of toilet that is common in Italy, flush (no pun intended) against the floor.
