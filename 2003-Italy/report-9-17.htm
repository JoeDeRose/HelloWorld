  • Our improvement at purchasing tickets for the train was eclipsed only by our improvement at getting on it:  We found our carriage and stowed our luggage with a minimum of frustration, and sat back to enjoy the ride to Stazione Milano Centrale.  The train made a pass through Bologna, and through the 18.5 km tunnel that was the site, in 1984, of one of Italy's worst terror attacks (when a train passing through the tunnel was bombed).  Our trip, of course, went without incident.
Train Ticket from Florence to Milan
  • We arrived in Milan and found our way to the Hotel Speronari, having been warned by the guidebook that it was without an elevator and – as a one-star hotel – was not the most luxurious of accommodations.  Warnings aside, the Speronari was fine for our purposes, and we loved its location and the easy access to Cokes and Coke Lights at its bar.  But we were taken aback by the bathroom:  The entire room was the shower, with a drain in the floor; a sink rested on rails above the toilet and bidet, and could be slid on those rails for access to whichever fixture was needed.  The Speroniari also provided our initiation to a system of lighting where the hallway lights are on timers, with a starter switch by the stairs and outside each room.

Bathroom at Hotel Speronari, with Sink Positioned for Access to Toilet

(The knob above the sink on the left controls water to the sink; the knob on the right controls the shower, on the wall adjacent to the sink.  Notice the shower drain between the toilet and the bidet [under the sink].  The shower curtain exists only to protect the mirror from splashing water.)

  • With a day to spend in Milan and no real agenda, the four of us trundled downstairs and over to the Piazza del Duomo, just behind the hotel.  We availed ourselves of the elevator access to the top of the Duomo (its full name is Duomo Santa Maria Nascente) so that Mom and Meridel could finally join us in exploring one of these spectacular rooftops.  Many people consider the Milan Duomo one of the finest examples of gothic architecture in the world; after seeing it, I am enthusiastically one of those people.

  • After the Duomo, Billy and I turned to our mounting problem with laundry, hauling it through the streets of Milan to a laundromat that the hotel recommended to us. Leaving Florence, I had come to think that I had overestimated my skill with Italian – but few people in Milan switched the conversation to English, and I found I was able to get along reasonably well in Italian.  In particular, the owner of the laundromat told me, "capisci bene" ("you understand well").  (Based on usage I found here and elsewhere, I took this as a compliment on my general skill, rather than a differentiation between my abilities of expression and comprehension).
  • We then returned to the Piazza del Duomo to see the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, an ornate covered shopping area facing the Piazza del Duomo.  We ran into Mom and Meridel eating here at a convenient table facing the main area, and then just looked around.

  • We retrieved our blessedly clean clothes and then, before it got too late, took the Milan subway to the Stazione Centrale to purchase our tickets to Venice for the next day.  Rick Steves said that after one ride on the Milan subway, we would look for excuses to use it.  The subway was adequate for our purposes, but I failed to swoon.
