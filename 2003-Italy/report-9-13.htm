  • I returned the vespa early the next morning and we set out by subway with Mom and Meridel to visit San Pietro in Vincoli to see Michelangelo's Moses (with horns because of a mistranslation of the Hebrew "rays of light") and the Chains of St. Peter.  (The miracle of the chains is – supposedly – that the two separate chains, which had been used to bind St. Peter, fused themselves spontaneously into one upon being brought to Rome.  But the way they are displayed in a glass-fronted box, hung from hooks that are out of view, there might be one, two, or ten chains in that box.  Anyway, pictures were forbidden, so you'll have to go to Rome to judge for yourself.)
  • We then took the subway a little farther south to see the Bocca della Verità in Santa Maria in Cosmedin, a large stone face whose mouth is said to snap shut on the hands of liars.  On the way there, we discovered that it sits across the street from the Temples of the Forum Boarium (the best preserved of the Roman Republican temples), and on the way back we got a spectacular view across the Circus Maximus of the Palace of Septimus Severus.

  • I missed the Sistene Chapel again because guidebook said it closed at 13:45, but Pope said it closed at 12:20.
  • So Billy and I returned to Trevi Fountain, and I threw in a coin so I could return to Rome and see the blasted chapel, and a second coin to find true love; I didn't throw in a third coin (to find true love in Rome) because that would have been too complicated and I would be back home soon enough.
  • Billy and I rechecked the guidebook to be sure we hadn't missed any of our main sites in Rome; finding that we had covered our itinerary, we took a long walk past sites we had already seen and did souvenir shopping.
  • Finally, we went to Stazione Termini and stood in four lines to buy train tickets to Florence – but the delay was my own fault.  Learning new ways to do things is one of the things I enjoy most about traveling, I reminded myself.
