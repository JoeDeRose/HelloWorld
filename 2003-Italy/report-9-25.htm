  • Our last day in Italy began at 3:45 AM, with a car waiting to take us to the airport for our absurdly early flight.  On the way we passed some of the sights we had seen before, notably the Bocca della Verità and the Circus Maximus.
  • Safely on Alitalia on the way to Brussels for our Delta flight, we passed out of Italy as we had entered it:  across the majestic Alps.  And on this pass across the Alps I had a nice surprise:  a glacier.  I had never seen a glacier before and had always wanted to see one.

  • The Brussels airport remained a challenge.  We knew where we were going this time, but it was still a long way there.  Because of post-September-11 security concerns, flights to the U.S. are clustered at the end of Terminal B.  Already having passed through security, one enters the U.S.-flight section through an area of additional security in which all carry-on baggage is unpacked and repacked, every single electronic device is activated, every shoe is scrutinized, and every passenger is patted down with surprising frankness.  Billy's insulin cooler caused some delay at this process as it was re-scanned in an x-ray machine (after having already been scanned twice, once in Italy and again at the concourse entrance in Brussels).  Then we arrived at the gate where we were first scolded for our late arrival (five minutes after the time at which they could have denied us entry, but didn't) and then subjected to a barrage of security questions more lengthy than any I had previously undergone.
  • The Brussels airport, however, was a minor distraction from a stupendous holiday.  I was exhausted from the trip, I had run out of clean clothes, I missed my dog, and I had blown my budget – but I would have been just as happy for that plane to turn around and point me back toward Rome.
