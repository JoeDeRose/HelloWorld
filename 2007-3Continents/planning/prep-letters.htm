Arabic Letters

If you want to know what those letters sound like, you can click on the controls below. Unlike, for example, the English letters H and W (to pick the two most prominent of many examples), Arabic letters always begin with the sound they make.

It is the like-shaded letters that students (who are natives speakers of English) find hardest to distinguish from each other. The two gray-shaded letters are sounds that are completely unlike sounds in English.
